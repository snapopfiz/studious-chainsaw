Modern Relative Stopping Effectiveness (MRSE)
I created this updated formula specifically for today's ammunition and real-world use. It builds on the spirit of Hatcher's RSP (momentum × area) but replaces outdated assumptions with measurable modern variables:

Expansion (critical for JHPs and modern defensive ammo)
Penetration (FBI 12–18" gel standard for reaching vitals)
Velocity and weight (energy deposition)
Shot placement (the single biggest real-world factor per Ellifritz's 1,800+ shooting study and FBI data)

The Formula (easy to calculate)
$$\text{MRSE} = \left( \frac{W \times V \times A_{\text{exp}}}{7000} \right) \times P_f \times PM$$
Variables (all publicly available from manufacturer gel tests or Lucky Gunner/FBI-style data):

$  W  $ = bullet weight in grains
$  V  $ = muzzle velocity in fps (from your barrel length)
$  A_{\text{exp}}  $ = expanded frontal area in square inches = $  \pi \times (D_{\text{exp}}/2)^2  $, where $  D_{\text{exp}}  $ = average expanded diameter in inches (from 10% ballistic gel)
$  P_f  $ = penetration factor (FBI-inspired efficiency)
$  P_f = 1.0  $ if penetration $  P  $ is 12–18 inches (ideal)
$  P_f = P / 12  $ if $  P < 12  $ (under-penetration penalty)
$  P_f = 18 / P  $ if $  P > 18  $ (over-penetration penalty)

$  PM  $ = placement multiplier (0–2.5 scale, based on real incapacitation data)

Why This Works Better Than Old Formulas

Hatcher RSP used original (non-expanding) diameter and ignored penetration → biased toward big slow FMJ.
Modern ammo (Gold Dot, HST, Speer, etc.) expands dramatically → larger wound channel + better energy dump.
Penetration factor enforces FBI gel standards (12–18" through barriers/clothing).
Placement multiplier acknowledges the truth: even a “weak” 9mm to the brain beats a .45 to the arm.
